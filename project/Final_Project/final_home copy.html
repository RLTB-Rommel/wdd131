Residential Solar

Refers to the installation and use of solar energy systems on homes or residential properties. These systems typically consist of photovoltaic (PV) panels mounted on the roof or ground, which capture sunlight and convert it into electricity through a process known as the photovoltaic effect. Residential solar systems are designed to offset a homeowner's reliance on traditional grid electricity by generating clean, renewable energy directly from sunlight.

Commercial/Industrial Solar

involves the deployment of solar energy systems on non-residential buildings and properties, such as businesses, factories, warehouses, and institutions. These installations are designed to harness solar power to meet the energy demands of large-scale operations and facilities.

Utility Scale Solar

Refers to large-scale solar energy projects developed specifically to generate electricity for utility companies and large power consumers. These installations typically involve extensive arrays of photovoltaic (PV) panels deployed over vast land areas or integrated into existing utility infrastructure.

Community Solar

Refers to solar energy projects developed to provide renewable electricity to multiple participants within a local community. These installations enable individuals, businesses, or organizations to benefit from solar power without needing to install panels on their own properties.
